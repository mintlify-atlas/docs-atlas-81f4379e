The `ai-protocol-kit` is the most complete single package in the MaRCoRe AI Protocol Hub. It bundles behavioral contracts and operational protocols that cover the full lifecycle of AI-assisted work — from shaping raw ideas into coherent artifacts, to reviewing outputs under adversarial pressure, to writing for real human readers, to keeping long-running AI sessions from silently falling apart. If you only pick up one repository from this hub, this is the one.

## What's Included

The kit is organized around four protocol categories, each targeting a different failure mode in AI-assisted workflows.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Shaping Protocols" icon="layer-group">
    Behavioral contracts for turning messy ideas into structured, workable artifacts. These protocols prevent AI sessions from wandering into premature solutions before the problem is actually understood.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Review Protocols" icon="magnifying-glass">
    Adversarial and structured review contracts that stress-test outputs, plans, and decisions. These protocols force the AI to surface gaps, weak logic, and missing constraints rather than rubber-stamp whatever was produced.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Writing Protocols" icon="pen-nib">
    Contracts that keep AI-generated writing grounded in the actual reader — their context, expectations, and tolerance for jargon. These protocols push back against AI's natural tendency to write for itself.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Operational Protocols" icon="gears">
    Session management and drift-prevention contracts for keeping long AI-assisted workflows coherent. These protocols handle handoffs, decision tracking, and context boundaries so nothing silently becomes canon that shouldn't be.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Who It's For

**Solo AI workers** who use ChatGPT, Claude, or Codex for real work — not demos, not experiments — and have watched a session drift sideways because there was no structure holding the AI's behavior in place. The kit gives you contracts you can load per-session and trust to hold.

**People building AI workflows** who need repeatable behavior from AI assistants across multiple sessions, projects, or collaborators. The kit's protocols are designed to be loaded, adapted, and reused — not read once and forgotten.

**Prompt engineers** who are tired of writing one-off system prompts for every new task. The protocols in this kit are already shaped by real use. Fork them, break them, rebuild them for your context. That's the point.

## Getting Started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Browse the kit on GitHub">
    Start at [github.com/XxYouDeaDPunKxX/ai-protocol-kit](https://github.com/XxYouDeaDPunKxX/ai-protocol-kit) and read the top-level README. It maps which protocol covers which problem so you're not guessing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Identify which protocols apply to your workflow">
    Match your current failure mode to the right category. Drifting sessions? Operational protocols. Outputs that sound good but fall apart under scrutiny? Review protocols. Writing that reads like a press release? Writing protocols.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Load the relevant protocol into your AI session">
    Paste the protocol contract into your system prompt, project instructions, or at the top of your session context. The contracts are written to be loaded directly — no translation required.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Adapt to your specific context">
    The protocols are starting points, not laws. Replace placeholder language with your actual project names, constraints, and outputs. A protocol that fits your workflow precisely will hold better than one loaded verbatim.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Start with one protocol at a time. Loading the entire kit into a single session context is a fast way to overload it. Pick the protocol that addresses your most pressing failure mode right now, run with it for a few sessions, and then add the next one once you know how the first one behaves in practice.
</Tip>
