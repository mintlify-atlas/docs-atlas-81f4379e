MaRCoRe is a lifelong geek and gamer who uses AI to shape messy ideas, workflows, and personal problems into practical tools and protocols. The projects collected here aren't built to fill a portfolio — they're built when an idea feels real, when it solves something specific, and when it's worth shaping into something other people can try, inspect, or reuse. Most of what you'll find falls into personal tools, AI protocols, and practical systems that came from real use before they were ever documented.

## Who This Is For

These projects are for people who work alongside AI assistants every day and have started to notice the cracks — sessions that lose track of decisions, responses that are either too shallow or overloaded, plans that evaporate between chats. The target user isn't necessarily a software developer. It's anyone who treats AI as a working partner: lifelong geeks, writers, researchers, people managing complex personal workflows, and anyone who has tried to use an AI to shape a messy idea into something usable.

If you've ever pasted the same context into a chat three times in a row, watched an AI flatten a nuanced constraint into a vague suggestion, or lost a decision thread halfway through a long project — these tools were built around exactly those problems.

## How These Projects Were Made

Every project in this hub was created through human-directed, AI-assisted work. The ideas, the practical structure, and the decisions about what problems to solve all came from real use — not from generating content to fill a page. AI tools were used during drafting, structuring, review, testing, and refinement, and that assistance is acknowledged openly rather than hidden.

That honesty matters here because the hub itself is about building systems that keep human direction intact when working with AI. The projects practice what they describe: the human sets direction, makes decisions, and owns the outcome. The AI assists.

<Note>
  These projects work best when you use them **with** an AI, not just read them as static documentation. Paste a repository link into your AI assistant, ask it to explain the project, show you examples, and tell you whether it fits your problem. That's usually the fastest way in.
</Note>
